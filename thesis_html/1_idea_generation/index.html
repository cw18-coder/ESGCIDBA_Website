Idea Generation Phase

Introduction

The idea generation phase marks the beginning of the doctoral research journey. This phase involves exploring potential research topics, identifying areas of interest, and beginning to shape the research focus through brainstorming and preliminary investigation.

Purpose

The primary purpose of this phase is to:

  • Explore multiple potential research areas
  • Identify gaps in existing knowledge
  • Consider the practical significance of potential topics
  • Assess the feasibility of various research directions
  • Develop initial research questions

Approach

The approach to idea generation involves:

  • Broad exploration: Casting a wide net to consider various business and management topics
  • Reflective practice: Considering personal and professional experiences that might inform research
  • Preliminary reading: Engaging with current literature to understand the state of research
  • Stakeholder consultation: Discussing ideas with advisors, peers, and industry professionals

Key Considerations

When generating and evaluating research ideas, several factors are considered:

Academic Contribution

Will this research contribute new knowledge or insights to the academic field? Does it address a genuine gap in existing literature?

Practical Relevance

As a DBA research project, the work should have practical implications for business practice. Will the findings be useful to practitioners, organizations, or policymakers?

Feasibility

Can this research be completed within the constraints of time, resources, and access? Are the necessary data sources available?

Personal Interest

Sustaining motivation throughout a multi-year research project requires genuine interest. Does this topic align with personal and professional interests?

Expected Outcomes

This phase should result in:

  • A shortlist of 2-3 viable research topics
  • Preliminary research questions for each topic
  • Initial understanding of existing literature in each area
  • Assessment of feasibility and potential contribution
  • A clear direction for the proposal phase

Moving Forward

The ideas developed in this phase will be refined and formalized in the proposal phase, where a specific research question, methodology, and theoretical framework will be developed in detail.
